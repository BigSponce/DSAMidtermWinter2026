*** Questions ***
1. Why was a Queue appropriate for the waiting room?
   A. It's the most appropriate as it follows First in, First out principles and because it works like a real waiting room in adding fairness. (first come first serve)

2. Explain FIFO Principle.
   A. FIFO, or First in, First out, is a valuation method that maintains the newest items while shunting out the oldest.

3. Explain what could happen if a stack was used instead of a queue in Part 1.
   A. A stack operates on a Last in, First out principle so the newest patient would always be seen first, and the first patient would be served last.

4. Suggest ways that you could improve this system (Theory)
   A. A database to store the records and previous entries into the waiting room would be an excellent addition. In the event some complication comes up, they staff can access that and work to another solution.

5. Why is a Doubly Linked List appropriate for patient history?
   A. The structure of a doubly linked list supports patient records by allowing forward and backward navigation.

6. What would happen in the DLL if you forgot to update the next and previous pointers when manipulating the DLL?
   A. Forgetting to update next and previous pointers can lead to a corruption of the data structure, causing problems down the road.

7. Explain why a Doubly Linked List Works in this case compared to an Array or Array List.
